Preparation Checklist

Verify you have all materials and tools from the guide before beginning installation.

Materials Required

  • 8x 16"x16" Step Stones
  • 1 bag Type S Mortar Mix (60–80 lb bag)
  • 1 tube Outdoor Adhesive (Optional alternative to mortar)

Tools Required

  • Trowel or Margin Trowel
  • Rubber Mallet & Small Level (12–24")
  • Stiff Brush, Sponge & Bucket
  • Safety Gear (Gloves & Eye Protection)

Installation Steps (5-Step Plan)

Follow the process step-by-step. Mark each step as complete to track your progress.

Step 1: Prep the Existing Base

▼
  1. Clean: Remove all loose debris, dirt, and any crumbling mortar from the current grill base area.
  2. Sweep/Vacuum: Ensure you expose solid stone or concrete beneath. A clean base is essential for mortar adhesion.

Step 2: Lay the Mortar Pads

▼
  1. Mix Mortar: Mix the Type S mortar to a thick, workable consistency (like **peanut butter**).
  2. Trowel Pads: For each of the four bottom stones, trowel four or five small, isolated pads of mortar onto the base (corners and center). **Avoid filling the entire area.**

Step 3: Set the First Layer (Stones 1–4)

▼
  1. Placement: Set the first four 16"x16" stones onto the mortar pads in a 2x2 square configuration.
  2. Level: Use the rubber mallet to gently tap each stone until they are flush and stable.
  3. Check: Use your level across the stones in all directions. Adjust any low points by adding or removing a small amount of mortar beneath the stone, then re-tapping.
  4. Wipe: Immediately wipe off any squeeze-out mortar with a damp sponge.

Step 4: Add the Second Layer (Stones 5–8)

▼
  1. Bonding: Apply a thin layer of mortar pads or a bead of construction adhesive directly onto the top of the first layer of stones.
  2. Stagger: Place the second set of four stones, slightly **staggering their seams** over the joints of the first layer for maximum structural stability.
  3. Final Level: Tap them down gently with the mallet and check the level across the new top surface.

Step 5: Reinstall the Grill

▼
  1. Cure Time: If you used mortar, wait at least 24 hours before placing the heavy grill back onto the riser.
  2. Ventilation: Ensure your grill has proper air circulation and clearance (the $\frac{3}{4}"$ gap around the original footprint is sufficient for most units).

Project Results: Total Lift $\approx 3\frac{1}{4}$". Stable, heat-safe, no cuts required.
